Lasagna (Classic Beef Lasagna)

Description

Lasagna is a layered Italian pasta dish featuring sheets of pasta, rich tomato sauce, ground meat, and creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese. Each layer melds together during baking, creating a warm, satisfying meal with a perfect blend of textures and flavors. Classic lasagna recipes use beef or sausage, while vegetable-based or seafood versions also bring delicious variety to the table.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef or Italian sausage
  • Onion (finely chopped)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Tomato paste
  • Ricotta cheese or béchamel sauce
  • Mozzarella cheese (shredded)
  • Parmesan cheese (grated)
  • Fresh basil and parsley
  • Salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning (oregano, thyme, basil)

Steps

  1. Prepare the meat sauce: In a skillet, cook ground beef or sausage with onion and garlic. Drain excess fat, then add tomato sauce, tomato paste, and seasonings (salt, pepper, Italian herbs). Let simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Prepare cheese mixture: In a bowl, mix ricotta or béchamel with shredded mozzarella, grated Parmesan, chopped basil, and parsley.
  3. Assemble lasagna: Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a baking dish. Layer lasagna noodles on top, followed by more meat sauce, then a layer of the cheese mixture. Repeat layers, ending with a cheese layer.
  4. Bake: Cover with foil and bake at 180°C (350°F) for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake an additional 15-20 minutes until golden and bubbly.
  5. Let rest: Allow the lasagna to cool for 10-15 minutes before slicing.
